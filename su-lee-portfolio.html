Systems in Practice

Two platforms addressing fundamental challenges in human development through structural, emotionally-intelligent design.

Say-It

AI-Based Reminiscence Architecture for Seniors

Problem Definition

As cognitive decline progresses, personal narratives fragment and disappear. Families lose access to generational wisdom. Current solutions treat memory preservation as a documentary task, ignoring the relational and emotional scaffolding required for meaningful reminiscence work.

Structural Solution

Say-It employs conversational AI to guide seniors through structured life review protocols, automatically organizing narratives into thematic and chronological memory architectures. The system adapts questioning strategies based on cognitive response patterns and emotional engagement levels.

Emotional Value

Beyond preservation, Say-It creates intergenerational bridges. It transforms isolated elderly experiences into structured legacies that families can engage with, understand, and carry forward. The system treats memory not as data, but as living connection.

Current Progress

Patent registered (September 2025). Functional prototype completed with full UI/UX design. User testing with elderly participants completed. Server implementation and production deployment in progress.

SelfTree

AI-Driven Self-Regulation Training System

Problem Definition

Elementary students face increasing demands for self-regulation without adequate developmental scaffolding. Traditional behavior management systems are reactive, punitive, and fail to build intrinsic regulatory capacity. The gap between neurological capability and environmental expectation continues to widen.

Structural Solution

SelfTree implements a developmental framework that treats self-regulation as a learnable skill system. AI-powered recognition identifies emotional states and behavioral patterns, providing real-time feedback loops that help children understand cause-effect relationships in their own regulation processes.

Emotional Value

Rather than imposing external control, SelfTree builds agency. Children develop metacognitive awareness of their emotional landscape and acquire practical tools for managing it. The system operates from a foundation of respect—treating young learners as capable architects of their own regulatory systems.

Current Progress

Two patents registered (January 2026). Functional prototype completed with full UI/UX design. System architecture validated. Preparing for pilot classroom deployment and longitudinal efficacy studies.

Why This Work Matters

Why connect memory and technology?
Memory is the substrate of identity. In an aging society, we face systematic erasure of generational knowledge and personal narrative. Technology should not replace human connection—it should create infrastructure for preserving it. Say-It exists because the stories that shape families deserve better than fragmented recollection and eventual loss.
Why design emotional systems?
Most educational technology treats learning as information transfer. But development is fundamentally emotional. Self-regulation, motivation, resilience—these are not add-ons to cognition; they are the foundation. Systems that ignore emotional architecture will always fail to create lasting change. Effective design requires understanding that feeling and thinking are inseparable.
How should AI serve human development?
AI should function as developmental scaffolding—temporary support structures that enable humans to build their own capacity. Not as replacement for human agency, but as intelligent infrastructure that adapts to individual needs, recognizes patterns we cannot, and offers feedback loops that accelerate growth. The measure of good AI is whether it makes people more capable, not more dependent.
